METAVERSE

IMPLEMENTATION

Components of metaverse technology have already been developed within online video games The eUniverse 1999 LivePlace “social interaction” platform or the 2003 virtual world platform Second Life are often described as the first metaverse, as it incorporated many aspects of social media into a persistent three-dimensional world with the user represented as an avatar, however historical claims of metaverse development started soon after the term was coined.

HARDWARE

Access points for the metaverse includes general-purpose computers and smartphones, augmented reality, mixed reality, and virtual reality . Dependence on VR technology has limited metaverse development and wide-scale adoption. Limitations of portable hardware and the need to balance cost and design have caused a lack of high-quality graphics and mobility. SOFTWARE

There has been no wide-scale adoption of a standardized technical specification for metaverse implementations, and existing implementations rely primarily on proprietary technology. Interoperability is a major concern in metaverse development, stemming from concerns about transparency and privacy. There have been several virtual environment standardization projects.
